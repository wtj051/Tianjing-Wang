Research identity
I develop trustworthy, physics-guided AI for clean-energy cyber-physical systems, with a focus on:
Safe grid operation: power-flow feasibility, voltage/security constraints, emergency control, and resilient grid operation.
Life-aware battery intelligence: SOH/SOC/RUL estimation, degradation-aware dispatch, and battery-to-grid decision support.
Collaborative energy intelligence: federated, privacy-preserving, and multi-agent learning for grid-battery-mobility systems.
My long-term goal is to build reproducible evaluation infrastructure and deployable AI methods that make energy systems safer, more durable, privacy-aware, and resilient.

Featured benchmark
TrustEnergyBench
A public benchmark for evaluating AI agents and foundation-model components in critical energy systems.
Initial tracks
SafeFlex: dynamic storage flexibility and safe grid operation under contingencies, renewable uncertainty, and EV charging demand.
BatteryLife: battery health-to-dispatch benchmarking, connecting SOH/RUL/future-capacity estimation to operational value and degradation risk.
CollaborativeEnergy: privacy-preserving multi-agent coordination for vehicles, charging stations, storage assets, and aggregators.
Evaluation dimensions
Prediction/decision accuracy
Power-flow and voltage/security constraint violations
SOC/SOH and degradation-risk constraint violations
Robustness to missing data, contingencies, renewable/EV uncertainty, and out-of-distribution operating states
Reproducibility across seeds, models, datasets, and simulator settings
